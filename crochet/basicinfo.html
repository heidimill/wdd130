Basic Information

As a beginner, sometimes it can be overwhelming to try to understand a lot of niche concepts. This page is meant to be a beginners guide.

These are the things I wish I knew when I started crochet.

Do different sized hooks really matter?

Short answer: yes

Some yarns do not respond well to smaller hooks and will begin to curl execively.

The different sizes of hooks also create different size projects.

Inline vs tapered crochet hooks:

For beginners, I suggest using inline hooks. They help with learning the yarn tension and will create more consistent stitches especially while first learning how to make the different stitches. The inline hook has a sharper point to insert between loops and a more consistent size throughout the hook with a deeper hook. The tapered hooks are more rounded and taper quite a bit near the hook.

Darning needles and stitch markers:

While regular sewing needles do work, sometimes it can be extremely difficult getting the yarn through the eye. Darning needles have a larger eye that is better for yarn and a duller tip that glides between the crochet stitches without catching. I recommend a darning needle.

A stitch marker could be convenient however most of the time using a piece of scrap yarn works just as well.
